Factor 01 Content Quality — The Ranking Factor That Overrides Everything

Google's entire business model depends on sending searchers to content that genuinely answers their questions. In 2026, after years of algorithm refinements, this remains the single most important ranking factor — not because it is the easiest to manipulate, but because it is the hardest to fake at scale. A blog post that truly, comprehensively, and clearly addresses what its target keyword's searcher needs will consistently outperform technically superior but genuinely thinner content over time.

📝

Write Content That Fully Satisfies Search Intent

Highest Impact

Before writing a single word, search your target keyword and study the top ten results carefully. What questions do they all answer? What structure do they share? What subtopics do they consistently include? This analysis reveals what Google has determined that searchers want — and your content needs to address all of it while adding something the existing results lack. Longer content ranks better not because of word count, but because thorough coverage of a topic naturally satisfies more of the related questions a searcher may have. Aim for depth over length: every paragraph should earn its place by serving the reader, not padding a word count.

Action Now

Open Google Search Console and find your posts ranking between positions 11–30. These articles are nearly ranking — they need targeted improvements to cross into page one, not new articles. Improve these posts first for the fastest ranking gains.

Factor 02 Backlinks — Votes of Trust From the Wider Web

🔗

Earn Links Rather Than Build Them — Quality Over Quantity

Off-Page Power

A backlink — another website linking to your content — is Google's most reliable signal that your content has earned external trust. The quality of the linking site matters enormously: a single link from a genuinely authoritative, relevant website carries more ranking weight than a hundred links from low-quality directories. The most sustainable way to earn backlinks is to create content so thorough, so original, or so data-rich that other writers naturally cite it as a reference. Guest posting on respected blogs in your niche, publishing original research or surveys, and creating comprehensive resource articles are the three most reliable ethical strategies for building the backlink profile that moves rankings consistently upward.

Action Now

Identify your three most thorough, most original pieces of content. These are your link-earning assets. Promote them to other bloggers in your niche, pitch them as references in relevant guest posts, and add them to your email newsletter to drive shares.

Factor 03 Page Experience — Google's Technical Report Card

⚡

Core Web Vitals — The Technical Signals Google Measures Directly

Technical

Since 2021, Google has used Core Web Vitals as direct ranking signals — measuring the speed, visual stability, and responsiveness of your pages as real users experience them. Largest Contentful Paint measures how quickly your main content loads. Cumulative Layout Shift measures how much page elements move unexpectedly as the page loads. Interaction to Next Paint measures how quickly your page responds to user input. These are not abstract technical scores — they directly reflect real user experience, and Google treats them as such. A blog failing Core Web Vitals faces a measurable ranking disadvantage against competitors whose pages score well, even when content quality is comparable.

Action Now

Run your blog through Google PageSpeed Insights at pagespeed.web.dev and check your Core Web Vitals field data. Focus first on compressing images, installing a caching plugin, and choosing a lightweight theme — these three changes typically resolve the majority of performance issues for WordPress blogs.

Factor 04 On-Page SEO — The Signals You Control Completely

🎯

Title Tags, Headings, and Keyword Placement Done Right

In Your Control

On-page SEO encompasses every element within your article that helps Google understand precisely what your content covers. Your title tag should include your primary keyword naturally and ideally within the first 60 characters. Your H1 heading, opening paragraph, and at least one H2 subheading should each contain the keyword or a close variant. Your URL slug should be short, descriptive, and keyword-rich. Images should have descriptive alt text. Internal links should connect related content across your blog, distributing authority and signalling topical depth. None of these require technical expertise — they require attention, and that attention, applied consistently to every piece of content you publish, compounds into meaningfully stronger rankings over time.

Action Now

Audit your five highest-traffic posts for on-page SEO. Check each one has the keyword in the title, URL, H1, opening 100 words, and at least one H2. Fix any gaps — these quick improvements can move existing rankings upward within days of reindexing.

Factor 05 E-E-A-T — The Human Trust Signals Google Increasingly Values

🏆

Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness and Trustworthiness

Growing Weight

E-E-A-T is Google's framework for evaluating whether content was created by someone with genuine experience and expertise on the topic — and whether that person and their website can be trusted. In 2026, with AI-generated content flooding the internet, human experience signals have become more valuable than ever. Add a detailed author bio demonstrating your credentials, link to authoritative external sources within your content, keep your About page specific and personal, earn mentions and links from respected voices in your niche, and — most importantly — write from direct experience wherever possible. The detail that comes from lived experience is precisely what Google's quality raters and algorithms are increasingly trained to recognise and reward.

Action Now

Update your About page to explicitly address your experience and credentials in your niche. Then add or update author bios on your most important posts. These are quick, free improvements that directly serve Google's E-E-A-T evaluation of your blog.
