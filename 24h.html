👁 Eye Wellness Guide

Eye Care Tips for Screen Users

Protect your most beautiful feature in a world lit by screens

Think about how many hours your eyes spend staring at screens today — your phone in the morning, a laptop at work, a tablet in the evening, and the television before bed. Our eyes were never designed for this much artificial light. Yet here we are, and our eyes are quietly paying the price. The good news? With a few simple, mindful habits, you can protect and care for your eyes every single day — and they will thank you for it.
👁️
Tip No. 1

Follow the 20-20-20 Rule

If there is one rule every screen user must know, it is this one. When we stare at screens, we blink far less than normal — sometimes only a third as often as we should. This causes eye muscles to tighten and the surface of the eye to dry out, leading to that familiar stinging, heavy feeling at the end of a long day.

Optometrists around the world recommend the 20-20-20 rule as the single most effective habit to prevent digital eye strain. It is beautifully simple and costs absolutely nothing.

Tip No. 2

Mind Your Screen Distance & Position

How you position yourself in front of a screen matters more than most people realise. Sitting too close forces your eye muscles to work overtime to focus, leading to headaches and fatigue. Sitting with a screen above eye level causes you to open your eyes wider, which accelerates drying.

📐
Golden rule: Keep your screen at arm's length — about 50 to 70 centimetres from your face. The top of the screen should sit at or slightly below eye level. This relaxed downward gaze naturally reduces how much of your eye surface is exposed, keeping moisture in and strain out.
◈ ◈ ◈
💡
Tip No. 3

Adjust Brightness & Reduce Blue Light

A screen that is significantly brighter than the room around it forces your eyes to constantly adjust, like walking from a dark room into harsh sunlight over and over again. Matching your screen's brightness to your environment takes enormous pressure off your eyes instantly.

Blue light — the short, high-energy wavelength emitted by all screens — is particularly problematic in the evenings. It suppresses the production of melatonin, disrupting your sleep, and has been linked to long-term retinal stress with prolonged exposure.

🌙
Simple fixes: Enable Night Mode or Warm Display on all your devices after 7 PM. Reduce screen brightness in dimly lit rooms. If you spend many hours at a computer professionally, consider investing in blue-light filtering glasses — they make a genuine difference to end-of-day eye comfort.
◈ ◈ ◈

Tip No. 4

Blink Consciously & Stay Hydrated

This sounds almost too simple — but it is one of the most overlooked eye care habits. Every time you blink, a fresh layer of tears spreads across the surface of your eye, keeping it clean, lubricated, and comfortable. Screen focus dramatically reduces blinking frequency, which is why eyes feel gritty and irritated after long sessions.

Hydration works from the inside out. When your body is dehydrated, tear production drops and eyes become dry and sensitive far more quickly. Many people who suffer from chronic dry eyes find significant relief simply by drinking more water throughout the day.

✨
Daily practice: Make a conscious effort to blink fully and slowly every few minutes when using screens. Keep a large glass of water at your desk as a constant reminder to hydrate. If you work in air-conditioned spaces — which accelerate eye dryness — preservative-free lubricating eye drops are a gentle, safe daily companion.
◈ ◈ ◈
🥗
Tip No. 5

Nourish Your Eyes From Within

True eye health begins on your plate. The retina and lens of your eyes rely on specific nutrients to stay healthy, fight oxidative damage from screen light, and maintain clear vision over time. Neglecting eye nutrition while staring at screens for hours is like exercising without ever eating — unsustainable.

🍽️
Eye-loving foods: Leafy greens like spinach and kale are rich in lutein and zeaxanthin — antioxidants that act as natural sunglasses for your retina. Carrots and sweet potatoes provide beta-carotene. Omega-3 fatty acids in walnuts and fish support tear quality. And Vitamin C in citrus fruits protects the lens from damage.

Daily Routine

Small Habits, Big Protection

Protecting your eyes is not a single act — it is a collection of small, daily decisions that add up to lifelong eye health.

😴

Screen-Free Sleep

Put all screens away at least 30 minutes before bed. Your eyes need darkness to fully rest and recover overnight.

🕶️

Wear UV Protection

Outdoors, always wear sunglasses with UV400 protection. UV damage to the eyes is cumulative and irreversible.

🌿

Palming Exercise

Rub your palms together, cup them over closed eyes, and breathe deeply for one minute. Instant soothing relief.

🩺

Regular Eye Checks

Visit an eye specialist at least once a year. Many conditions are silent at first — early detection saves vision.
