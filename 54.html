Understand First What "Free to Use" Actually Means — Licence Types Explained

Before diving into specific websites, it is worth understanding what you are actually allowed to do with free stock images — because "free" does not always mean "use for anything." Most of the best sites operate under Creative Commons Zero (CC0) licences, which grant you the widest possible freedom. Others have custom licences with specific restrictions worth knowing before you use an image commercially.

⚖️   Free Image Licence Types — What Each Allows
CC0 — Use for anything, no attribution required, commercial use permitted
CC BY — Free to use commercially but must credit the photographer
Custom Free — Site-specific licence, usually similar to CC0 but check details
Editorial Only — Free to use but not for commercial or promotional purposes

Site 01 Unsplash — The Gold Standard of Free Photography

01 🏆

Unsplash — unsplash.com

Free Commercial Use Premium Quality

Unsplash has fundamentally changed what people expect from free stock photography. Its collection of over five million high-resolution photographs — contributed by a global community of professional and semi-professional photographers — covers virtually every subject a blogger could need, and the editorial and aesthetic quality is consistently exceptional. Lifestyle, architecture, food, travel, technology, people, nature, abstract — the breadth is extraordinary and the quality almost uniformly excellent.

The Unsplash Licence is generous and permissive: you can use images for free in commercial and non-commercial projects, modify and adapt them, and are not required to attribute the photographer — though attribution is strongly encouraged as a matter of professional courtesy. The only restriction is that you may not sell Unsplash images as standalone files or compile them into competing stock image platforms. For blogs, this means complete freedom to use any image in any post, any social media graphic, any newsletter, or any branded visual you create.

Best For

Featured blog images, hero sections, social media graphics, and any content where the highest visual quality is a priority.

Site 02 Pexels — The Most Diverse Collection

02 🌍

Pexels — pexels.com

Free Commercial Use Largest Library

Pexels offers one of the largest free image libraries available anywhere online — and critically, it curates both photographs and videos under its own completely free licence. Where Unsplash tends toward a certain editorial aesthetic that occasionally feels homogenous, Pexels is notably more diverse in both subject matter and photographic style, making it particularly valuable for bloggers in niches like food, family, health, fitness, and business who need images that genuinely reflect real people in real situations rather than carefully staged lifestyle photography.

The Pexels Licence, like Unsplash, allows free use in commercial and non-commercial projects without attribution requirements — though crediting creators is always appreciated and professionally courteous. Pexels has also built a particularly strong video library, making it the first destination for bloggers who need free video clips for their content or social media output.

Best For

Niches requiring diverse representation, realistic lifestyle imagery, and any blogger also creating video content who needs a single destination for both.

Site 03 Pixabay — The All-in-One Creative Resource

03 🎨

Pixabay — pixabay.com

Free Illustrations Too Video & Music

Pixabay's greatest strength is its breadth — it is not purely a photography platform. With over four million assets spanning photographs, vector illustrations, digital art, icon packs, and video footage, it is the most versatile free creative resource available to bloggers. The illustration and vector library is particularly distinctive: for bloggers who need infographic elements, digital artwork, or concept-driven visuals that photographs cannot convey, Pixabay's illustration collection is genuinely unmatched among free platforms.

All Pixabay content is released under the CC0 licence — completely free for commercial use, no attribution required, no restrictions on modification. The search and filtering tools are among the most sophisticated on any free platform, allowing you to filter by orientation, size, colour palette, and content type with precision that saves significant time when searching for specific visual requirements.

Best For

Bloggers needing illustrations and vectors alongside photographs — infographic content, technology topics, and abstract concept visuals that stock photos rarely capture well.

Site 04 StockSnap.io — Curated Quality Over Quantity

04 ✨

StockSnap.io — stocksnap.io

Free CC0 Curated Commercial Use

StockSnap prioritises curation over volume — its library is smaller than Pexels or Pixabay, but every image it contains has been reviewed for quality before acceptance. For bloggers who find the larger platforms overwhelming or who find themselves scrolling through too many mediocre results, StockSnap's editorial approach produces a noticeably higher hit rate when searching for something genuinely beautiful. New images are added daily and sorted by trending, which surfaces the freshest and most popular content prominently, making it an excellent destination when you need inspiration rather than a specific search term in mind. All content is CC0.

Best For

Bloggers who value editorial quality and want a curated experience without sifting through thousands of mediocre results to find one exceptional image.

Site 05 Reshot — The Authentic Alternative

05 🌱

Reshot — reshot.com

Free Anti-Generic Commercial Use

Reshot positions itself explicitly as an antidote to the visual clichés that dominate mainstream stock photography — no staged business handshakes, no smiling professionals pointing at laptops, no multicultural teams arranged in ascending height order. Instead, Reshot curates genuinely authentic, documentary-style photographs that feel like real moments captured rather than scenes constructed. For bloggers whose niche involves authenticity — personal finance, mental health, lifestyle, sustainability, community — Reshot's visual vocabulary resonates in ways that more polished stock photography often cannot.

Best For

Personal blogs, wellness content, and any niche where authentic human moments are more compelling than professional photography studio aesthetics.
